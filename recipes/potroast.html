Instant Pot Mississippi Pot Roast

A delicious, highly addictive meal!

This is one of my favorite recipes to make in the instant pot. The meat is so tender and juicy, and it explodes with flavor with every bite. Hard to resist, and addictive once you've tried it! Grab your ingredients and your instant pot, and let's make one of the best meals you'll ever have!

Ingredients:

  • 3-6 lbs. Chuck Roast, cut into 1 inch chunks
  • 3 tablespoons Vegetable or Olive Oil
  • 1 Small Onion, chopped
  • 9 Pepperoncinis, whole or chopped
  • 1/2 cup pepperoncini juice from the jar
  • 3/4 cup Low Sodium Beef Broth (or simply use water)
  • 1 (1 oz.) packet Dry Ranch Dressing Mix
  • 1 (1 oz.) packet Au Jus Gravy Mix
  • 1/2 - 1 Stick of Butter, unsalted

For Gravy:

  • 3 tablespoons Flour
  • 3 Tablespoons Butter, unsalted

Directions:

  1. Turn Pot on Saute mode. Add oil when hot.
  2. When oil heats up, place meat in one layer into the pot and sear/brown each side for 5 minutes.
  3. Remove meat to foil or covered dish. Wrap to keep it warm.
  4. Deglaze the pot: Add onion to pot and stir, add a bit of beef broth to loosen the brown bits from the bottom of the pot. Stir, scraping the bottom of the pot until all stuck on brown bits are free.
  5. Add pepperoncinis and thier juice, the remaining beef broth, au jus and ranch packets, and butter. Stir to combine.
  6. Turn off Saute mode and add meat back into the pot.
  7. Close and lock the lid and set the steam release vale to "Sealing."
  8. Set the pot to "Manual" mode and set the time for 60 minutes.
  9. When the cooking cycle ends, let the pressure naturally release for 15 minutes.
  10. After 15 minutes of natural pressure release, quick release remaining pressure by turning the steam release valve to "Venting."

Make the Gravy:

  1. Remove the roast to a plate, shred and cover.
  2. Turn the Saute function back on.
  3. Mix the flour and butter together until well combined.
  4. When the liquid is starting to simmer in the pot, add the mixture, stirring well until thickened.
  5. Turn off pot and add roast back in to the gravy.
  6. Enjoy with mashed potatoes, or over rice.
